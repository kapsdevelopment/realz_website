C2PA-compatible exports

Content Credentials for verified photo exports

Realz is capture-first. The proof starts when a photo is taken inside the app. When you export a verified photo with Content Credentials enabled, Realz adds C2PA-compatible metadata so provenance travels with the image file.

Best fit

Verified photo exports for publishing, documentation, review, and professional workflows where the image file itself should carry a readable provenance signal alongside the public Realz proof.

How C2PA fits the Realz proof model

Realz creates a proof around the captured photo bytes. When a user shares or exports a verified photo, we keep the Realz verification page as the primary proof surface. For exports with Content Credentials enabled, we also embed C2PA-compatible metadata inside the JPEG file.

What the export includes

  • A Realz proof URL that people can open independently.
  • The Realz proof identifier for the verified capture.
  • Integrity metadata that connects the export back to the Realz proof.
  • Creator or copyright fields when configured by the user.

Why this is useful

Content Credentials make Realz exports easier to inspect in tools that understand image provenance metadata. That matters when a file moves through publishing, reporting, documentation, or review workflows where a plain image can quickly lose context.

What remains canonical

The Realz verification page remains the canonical proof surface. Some social platforms, messaging apps, and image pipelines strip metadata. The verify URL gives people a separate place to check proof state even when embedded metadata does not survive distribution.

What Realz does not claim

Content Credentials do not prove every fact inside a scene, and they do not turn an old imported file into a Realz capture. They carry provenance information for Realz-verified exports while the underlying Realz proof explains the capture and verification path.

Questions people ask

Does Realz claim C2PA certification?

No. We describe this as C2PA-compatible Content Credentials. We will not claim C2PA certification, formal conformance, or trusted-provider status unless that process has been completed.

Does C2PA slow down taking the photo?

No. Turning on C2PA for exports does not slow down the capture moment. Realz creates the capture proof first, then adds C2PA-compatible Content Credentials when the verified image is exported or shared.

Realz layers

  • Capture in the Realz app.
  • Create a signed proof for the captured bytes.
  • Share a public Realz verify URL.
  • Optionally export a JPEG with C2PA-compatible Content Credentials.
