Use case

Social sharing with trusted photo proof

Social feeds are full of images people have to interpret on sight. Realz helps users share moments with a verification link, so others are not left guessing what was edited, filtered, imported, or synthetic.

Best fit

Public posts where a user wants to say: this is not just an image I uploaded. This is a photo I captured in Realz and shared with proof.

Why social sharing needs better authenticity signals

Visual posts can be persuasive even when they are edited or generated. Realz gives people a way to attach a public proof to a real captured moment, making the verification surface separate from likes, captions, and platform assumptions.

How Realz helps

  • Capture the image in Realz instead of importing it later.
  • Create a cryptographic proof for the photo.
  • Share a public verification link anywhere the image appears.
  • Let other people inspect the Realz proof without installing the app.

What Realz does not claim

Realz does not make every social media claim true. It gives a stronger signal that a shared photo was captured in Realz and preserved through proof creation.

Practical workflow

Capture in Realz, create the proof, and share the verification link alongside the photo. Anyone who sees the post can open the Realz verification page to check the proof state.

C2PA-compatible Content Credentials

When sharing or exporting a verified image, Realz can add embedded, C2PA-compatible Content Credentials. Some social platforms may strip metadata, so the Realz verification link remains the durable way to inspect the proof.

Questions people ask

Is Realz only for serious evidence?

No. Realz can also be useful for normal people who want to share a real moment with a stronger authenticity signal.

Can platforms strip Realz proof?

A platform may change uploaded image files, but the Realz verification link remains a separate public proof surface.

Use Realz when

  • You want to share a real moment with proof.
  • A photo might be mistaken for AI-generated content.
  • Trust matters more than polish.
